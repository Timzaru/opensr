Informed Consent

Assessing Attitudes and Occupations with Implicit and Explicit Measures.

The purpose of this research project is to gather information about attitudes people have about different occupations. This research project will have two primary sections. First, you will be asked to sort words into categories on the computer screen. Although the instructions for sorting will vary, the task remains the same. Second, you will be asked to complete a questionnaire. The questionnaire will ask you about your perceptions of personality traits, your opinion about different occupations, and demographic information such as your gender, age, education, and occupation.

This research project is experimental in nature. Participants will be divided into different groups, and each group may be given different instructions, or may complete questionnaires in a different order. All of the tasks you are being asked to complete should take approximately 20 minutes. Your time is greatly appreciated!

There is no anticipated risk in your participation. Although you may not receive direct benefit from your participation, others may ultimately benefit from the knowledge obtained from this research.

All information obtained in this study is strictly confidential unless disclosure is required by law. In addition, the Institutional Review Board and University or government officials responsible for monitoring this study may inspect these records. You will not be asked to provide your name or any other identifying information. The only potentially identifying information is the computer that you are using; however, your IP address is not being logged or saved. If you have any questions or concerns, please contact the primary investigator or dissertation advisor.

Your participation in this project is voluntary. Even after you agree to participate in the research, you may decide to leave the study at any time without penalty.
